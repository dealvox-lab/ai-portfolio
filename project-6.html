01 THE CHALLENGE

A Benefits Administration Platform faced a massive annual bottleneck during Open Enrollment cycles: processing benefits plan updates required manually interpreting thousands of unstructured documents and rewriting benefit rules into structured XML format:

  • Multi-Format Chaos: Updates arrived as PDFs, PowerPoint slides, emails, DOC files, spreadsheets‚Äîeach with different layouts
  • Manual Interpretation: Human ops teams spent weeks reading plan updates and determining what changed year-over-year
  • XML Rewriting Burden: Every change required manual translation from human-readable text to machine-readable XML rules
  • Inconsistent Formatting: Different cities, employers, and plan providers used wildly different document structures
  • Scalability Limits: Thousands of historical documents needed processing, but manual workflow couldn't scale
  • Knowledge Dependency: Understanding "how humans read benefits updates" required domain expertise that was siloed in a few team members

The company needed an AI system that could automatically detect, interpret, and convert annual benefits plan changes from unstructured documents into structured XML outputs‚Äîprocessing thousands of documents with accuracy while reducing human workload on repetitive "diff-and-rewrite" tasks.

Key Outcomes

The Benefits Document Intelligence System transformed annual benefits processing from a manual, time-intensive bottleneck into an intelligent, scalable AI pipeline. By combining supervised learning, custom LLM training, and automated XML generation, the solution processes thousands of unstructured documents across wildly different formats‚Äîdelivering structured, machine-readable outputs that feed directly into benefits administration platforms.

The supervised learning approach using Labelbox created a training dataset that teaches the AI not just text extraction, but genuine understanding of how humans interpret benefits changes. This domain knowledge, combined with robust preprocessing and validation layers, ensures consistent performance across different cities, employers, and document structures. The human-in-the-loop review workflow handles edge cases while the AI handles the repetitive bulk work, creating an optimal division of labor that scales with business growth.
